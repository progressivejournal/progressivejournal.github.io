Concept-SAE: A Controllable and Invertible Concept Interface for Sparse Autoencoders

Jianrong Ding, Muxi Chen, Chenchen Zhao, and Qiang Xu

Progressive Journal · 2026

This paper introduces Concept-SAE, a framework that extends sparse autoencoders with a structured and controllable interface for probing user-defined concepts. The method decomposes an activation subspace into semantically aligned Concept Tokens and residual Free Tokens, enabling both targeted concept analysis and open-ended feature discovery.

Concept-SAE uses supervision on concept existence and spatial localization to produce faithful, localized, and disentangled concept representations. The paper further evaluates the framework through adversarial sample detection, controllable counterfactual editing, and stability diagnosis under adversarial perturbations.
